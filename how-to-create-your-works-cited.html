Week 11 • The Literary Research Essay

How to Create Your Works Cited

A step-by-step guide to building, formatting, and finalizing your Works Cited page

Before You Begin

Have the following open and ready before you start working through these steps:

  • Your completed Secondary Sources Template — your three secondary source MLA citations are already there
  • Your research essay document, open and ready to edit
  • The Works Cited Entries for Your Short Story Research Essay page — you will need it for Step 3
Follow Along with Jordan Rivera

Jordan is writing a literary analysis essay about Zora Neale Hurston’s short story “Sweat.” She has already completed her Secondary Sources Template and has three secondary source citations ready to go. At each step below, a Jordan’s Example section shows exactly what her Works Cited looks like at that stage. Follow along to see how a real Works Cited page comes together from start to finish.

1 Copy Your Secondary Source Citations

Open your completed Secondary Sources Template. Your three MLA citations are listed there, each under its own source section. For each source, select and copy the MLA Citation and then paste it into your research essay document to begin building your Works Cited. Paste all three entries one after another — you will alphabetize them in the next step.

Tip: Paste the entries at the very end of your essay document, after your final paragraph. You may want to add a page break first so the Works Cited starts on its own page.

2 Alphabetize Your Entries

You now have three secondary source citations in your document. Arrange them in alphabetical order by the author’s last name. If an entry has no author, alphabetize it by the first significant word of the article title.

Note: If the first word of an authorless title is “A,” “An,” or “The,” skip it and alphabetize by the next word instead.

3 Add Your Primary Source Entry

Your primary source — the short story you wrote about — also needs a Works Cited entry. Open the Works Cited Entries for Your Short Story Research Essay page and follow the instructions there to locate and copy the pre-formatted entry for your story.

1.   Locate the author who wrote the story your essay is about.
2.   Copy only the Works Cited entry for that author.
3.   Paste it into your Works Cited alongside your three secondary source entries.
4.   Place it in the correct alphabetical position.

Note: After pasting, check whether the entry pasted with a URL formatted as a blue hyperlink. If so, you will fix that in Step 4.

4 Fix URL Formatting

When you paste entries from a database, URLs often arrive with two separate problems. Check for both:

Problem: Empty gaps appearing before or within a URL

Long URLs sometimes create an awkward blank space on the line just before them. See Video 2 on the MLA Documentation: The Works Cited page for the technique to fix this.

Problem: URL appears as blue underlined hyperlink text

MLA requires URLs to appear as plain black text, not as clickable links.

Fix: Right-click the hyperlink and select Remove Hyperlink. Then confirm the text color matches the rest of the entry.
Jordan’s Example

All four of Jordan’s entries pasted with blue hyperlinked URLs. She right-clicks each one and selects Remove Hyperlink to convert them to plain black text.

5 Apply Hanging Indents

Every Works Cited entry requires a hanging indent: the first line of the entry sits at the left margin, and every line after it is indented half an inch.

Manual method

Place your cursor at the beginning of each continuation line and press Tab once to indent it.

Automatic method (recommended)

Use your word processor’s Hanging Indent paragraph formatting option. Video 1 on the MLA Documentation: The Works Cited page demonstrates this step by step.

Note: Applying indents can shift URL spacing again. After this step, go back and check Step 4 to make sure URL gaps and hyperlinks are still corrected.

Jordan’s Example

Jordan selects all four entries and applies the Hanging Indent paragraph option. Every second line and beyond shifts in by half an inch.

6 Adjust Spacing

The Works Cited page must be double-spaced throughout — both within each entry and between entries. Select all the text on the Works Cited page and apply double spacing.

Common mistake: extra blank lines between entries

Do not insert a blank line between entries to create visual separation. The space between entries should look identical to the space between lines within a single entry.

Fix: Delete any blank lines you find between entries. Select all and apply double spacing as a single formatting pass.

7 Italicize Journal, Database, and Website Titles

When you paste bibliographic information from a database, the original italics are often stripped. Go through each entry carefully and italicize the following types of titles if they appear:

Journal and magazine titles

e.g., The English Journal, Short Story Criticism

Database titles

e.g., JSTOR, EBSCOhost, Gale Literature: LitFinder

Anthology and collection titles

e.g., The Oxford Book of American Short Stories, LitFinder Contemporary Collection

Reminder: Article and short story titles go in quotation marks, not italics. Only the containing publication, anthology, or database name is italicized.

Jordan’s Example

Jordan’s database entries lost their italics when she pasted them. She goes through each entry and italicizes the journal, database, anthology, and collection titles. All four entries have at least one title to italicize.

8 Add the Works Cited Heading

At the very top of the page, type the words Works Cited. Then apply the following formatting to the heading:

✓ Centered

Align it to the center of the page

✗ Not bold

Same weight as the entry text

✗ Not underlined

Plain text only

✗ Not in quotes

Write it without quotation marks

✅ Final Checklist — Before You Submit Your Essay
Run through each item before submitting:
☐   Titled “Works Cited” — centered, not bolded, not in quotes
☐   All URLs are plain black text — no blue hyperlinks
☐   Entries in alphabetical order by author’s last name
☐   Font and size match the rest of the essay
☐   Entire page is double-spaced — no extra blank lines
☐   Four entries total: one primary source + three secondary sources
☐   Hanging indent applied to all entries
☐   Every source cited in the essay appears on the Works Cited — and vice versa
