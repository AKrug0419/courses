📝 Assignment Instructions

If you haven't yet reviewed the database demonstration videos on the previous page — Using Academic Databases for Literary Research — please stop now and complete that page first. The steps shown in this assignment will not make sense without that foundation.

Once you've reviewed the demonstration videos, complete the three steps below in order:

Step 1
Locate three secondary sources using Valencia's academic databases

Using Valencia's literary criticism databases, locate three secondary sources related to the short story you've chosen to write about. Your sources must come from the college's library databases — not the open internet.

Remember: your sources must be scholarly articles or criticism that analyzes or interprets your specific short story — not biographical articles about the author, plot summaries, or articles about different works.

⚠️ Use the Databases — Not Google

Do not search Google or any general internet site for your secondary sources. Articles found outside the college's academic databases will not be accepted, and you will need to redo the assignment. The demonstration videos on the Using Academic Databases page show you exactly where and how to search.

Step 2
Collect bibliographic information using the Citation Tool

In order to cite your secondary sources in the essay and list them on your Works Cited page, you'll need accurate bibliographic information for each one. Using the Citation Tool built into each database, locate the required information for all three sources and record it on the Secondary Sources Template exactly as demonstrated in the video below.

📄 Secondary Sources Template

Download the Secondary Sources Template and fill it in with the required bibliographic information for each of your three secondary sources, using the database's Citation Tool as shown in the demonstration video below. 📋 Evaluation
📋 How This Is Graded

There is no rubric for this assignment. Your submission will be reviewed to confirm that you have three credible secondary sources from Valencia's academic databases, along with all of the required bibliographic information requested on the Secondary Sources Template. Any missing or inaccurate information will be noted directly on your document when it is assessed.

💡 Formative Assessment

This is a formative assessment: as long as you complete the assignment with three sources from the college databases, you'll receive full credit. If there are any concerns — a source that doesn't qualify, missing citation fields, or a source that strays outside the acceptable scope — feedback and suggestions will be provided directly on your template.

⚖️ Academic Integrity

Valencia College is dedicated to engaging in intellectual activity and maintaining academic standards that include a shared commitment to transparency, integrity, and creating the right conditions for learning. All work submitted must reflect your own original research and reading. Valencia Policy 6Hx28:8-11: Academic Dishonesty ↗

Original Work

The sources you submit must be ones you located yourself through the college's databases. Submitting sources found by someone else or generated by AI constitutes a violation of academic integrity.

Consequences

Violations may result in a zero for this assignment. Students have one week to respond to any allegation of academic dishonesty before a penalty is assigned.

🤖 AI & Technology Policy for This Assignment
✅ Grammarly — Permitted (with limits)

You may use Grammarly to identify grammar and punctuation errors only. Use the "Correction" option only — do not use "Clarity," "Engagement," or "Delivery," as these rewrite your sentences and constitute a violation.

❌ AI Generators — Not Permitted

Do not use ChatGPT, Gemini, or similar tools to locate sources, generate citation information, or complete any part of the template. AI tools cannot reliably produce accurate bibliographic data and their use here violates academic integrity.

❌ Fabricated Citations — Not Permitted

The bibliographic information on your template must be drawn directly from the database Citation Tool — not guessed at or invented. Inaccurate citation information will require you to locate the correct information and resubmit.

🔍 Canvas AI Report

Your instructor has access to Canvas's AI Report feature (separate from Turnitin). If your submission generates a flag, you will be asked to redo the assignment — in part or in whole — depending on what the report identifies.
