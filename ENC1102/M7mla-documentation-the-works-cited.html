The Works Cited

Building, formatting, and cleaning up your Works Cited entries

Overview

What the Works Cited Is — and Why It's Annoying

Prepare to be annoyed.

Creating a Works Cited for a research essay is frustrating. Worse, teachers are allowed to make it worth as much of the essay's grade as they want — so too many errors on it can make your grade cry.

The Works Cited serves a very important function: it provides all of the bibliographic information about every source cited inside your essay — both your primary source (the poem) and your secondary sources. Fortunately, a significant portion of the tedious work was already done for you during the Source Log Assignment, where you used each database's Citation Tool to collect bibliographic information for your secondary sources in MLA Works Cited format.

The main challenge isn't the entries themselves — it's that formatting errors almost always appear when you transfer those entries from the Source Log into your essay document. Spacing shifts, indentations get lost, URLs become hyperlinks, and punctuation disappears. The problems section below walks through exactly how to recognize and fix each issue. Transfer Problems

What Goes Wrong When You Transfer Entries — and How to Fix It

When you copy and paste your Works Cited entries from the Source Log into your essay document, the transfer almost never comes through cleanly. Below are the six most common problems to check for after pasting.

Problem 1 — Hanging Indent Missing

MLA Works Cited entries use a hanging indent: the first line of each entry starts at the left margin, and all subsequent lines are indented half an inch. This formatting is almost always lost when you paste entries in.

Looks wrong: All lines flush to the left margin — no indentation at all.
Fix: Select the entry, open the paragraph formatting menu, and set a hanging indent of 0.5". The first video below demonstrates this step by step.
Problem 2 — Double Spacing Lost or Added

The Works Cited page must be double-spaced throughout — both within each entry and between entries. No extra blank lines between entries, and no single-spaced lines within them.

Looks wrong: Single-spaced entries, or large gaps where an extra blank line was inserted between them.
Fix: Select all text on the Works Cited page and apply double spacing. Delete any blank lines between entries.
Problem 3 — URLs Are Hyperlinked

When a URL is pasted into a Word document, it automatically becomes a clickable blue hyperlink. MLA format requires URLs to appear as plain black text, not styled hyperlinks.

Looks wrong: URL appears in blue underlined text.
Fix: Right-click the link and select "Remove Hyperlink." Then check that the text color matches the rest of the entry. The second video below covers this in detail.
Problem 4 — Long URLs Breaking Awkwardly

Long web addresses sometimes push a line so far to the right that it creates a large blank space in the middle of the entry, making it look incorrectly formatted.

Looks wrong: A line that ends after one or two words with a large gap before the URL continues on the next line.
Fix: The second video below demonstrates the specific technique for handling long URL line breaks.
Problem 5 — Font or Size Mismatch

Pasted text often brings its own font and size. Your Works Cited entries may end up in a different typeface or size than the rest of your essay — a clear formatting violation.

Looks wrong: Works Cited entries appear in a slightly different font, size, or weight than the essay body.
Fix: Select all the entries and apply your essay's standard font and size — Times New Roman 12pt for MLA.
Problem 6 — Citation Tool Errors

Database Citation Tools are good but not perfect. They occasionally generate entries with missing punctuation, incorrect capitalization, or the wrong format for a specific source type.

Looks wrong: A missing period at the end of an entry, all-caps title, or an obviously blank or placeholder field.
Fix: Compare each entry against the handout's models and correct any discrepancies by hand.

A complete Works Cited for this essay will have three entries: one for your poem and one for each of your two secondary sources.

The entries must appear in alphabetical order by the author's last name. If a source has no individual author listed (for example, a website page attributed only to an organization), alphabetize by the first word of the title, ignoring articles like "A," "An," and "The."

Poetry-Specific Note

Poem titles go in quotation marks. Collection titles (like Ariel or Selected Poems) are italicized. Website names (like Poetry Foundation) are also italicized.

Before You Submit

Final Works Cited Checklist

Run through each item below before submitting your essay. Every checked box is a formatting error you won't lose points for.

Works Cited — Final Check
Titled Works Cited — centered at the top, not bolded, not in quotation marks
Entries in alphabetical order by author's last name (or title, if no author)
Entire page is double-spaced — no extra blank lines between entries
Hanging indent applied to all entries (first line flush left; subsequent lines indented 0.5")
All URLs are plain black text — no blue underlined hyperlinks
Font and size match the rest of the essay (Times New Roman 12pt)
Three entries total: your poem + two secondary sources
Every source cited in the essay appears on the Works Cited — and vice versa
