Sweat, Serpents, and Survival: Domestic Violence and
Literary Symbolism in Hurston's "Sweat"3

Zora Neale Hurston's short story "Sweat" depicts the struggle of Delia Jones, a washerwoman whose life is defined by her abusive husband Sykes and the domestic labor she performs to sustain their household.4 Throughout the story, Hurston uses the recurring symbols of sweat, serpents, and the domestic space of the home to argue that Delia's labor is both the source of her oppression and, ultimately, the instrument of her liberation.

The most prominent symbol in the story is the rattlesnake that Sykes introduces into the household.5 Carter establishes a clear parallel between the snake's venom and Sykes's cruelty, suggesting that both represent the same patriarchal violence that has poisoned Delia's domestic life from the beginning.

▸ Formatting Rules
1
Last Name & Page Number (Header)
▼

In the header of your Word document, type your last name followed by a space and the page number. Set this to align to the right margin. Use the automatic page number feature so it increments on every page.

This header appears on every page of your essay — including the first page.

How it looks Ramirez 1

In Word: Insert → Header → Edit Header → type your last name and a space, then Insert → Page Number → Current Position → Plain Number.

2
Heading Block (Top of Page 1 Only)
▼

At the very top of the first page — not a separate title page — type these four lines in order, each on its own line, aligned to the left margin:

Format Your First and Last Name
Professor Holliday
ENC1102
Day Month Year

For the date, write it in Day Month Year order with no commas: 14 March 2025, not March 14, 2025.

This block is double-spaced like the rest of the document — do not add extra blank lines between items.

3
Title
▼

The title goes on the line immediately after the heading block — no extra blank line. Center it. Use standard title case (capitalize major words).

Do not bold, italicize, underline, enlarge, or put quotation marks around your own title — unless the title contains the name of another work, which gets its normal formatting (italics for long works, quotation marks for short ones).

Correct Sweat, Serpents, and Survival: Domestic Violence and Literary Symbolism in Hurston's "Sweat"
4
Body Text & Double Spacing
▼

The entire essay — heading block, title, and body — is double-spaced throughout. Do not add extra space between paragraphs or after the title. The line spacing should be exactly 2.0, not 1.5 or "At least."

Font: Times New Roman, 12pt is standard and recommended for MLA. Margins should be 1 inch on all sides.

In Word: Select All (Ctrl+A) → Home → Line and Paragraph Spacing → 2.0. Then go to Line Spacing Options and make sure "Don't add space before/after paragraphs" is checked.

5
Paragraph Indentation
▼

The first line of every body paragraph is indented 0.5 inches (one Tab press). This applies to every paragraph including the first one — there is no exception for the opening paragraph.

Do not use the space bar to create an indent. Use the Tab key, or set a first-line indent in Word's paragraph formatting settings.

The heading block and title are not indented — only your body paragraphs.

⚙️ General Formatting Checklist
🔤
Font
Times New Roman, 12pt. Use this throughout — heading block, title, and body.
📐
Margins
1 inch on all four sides. This is usually the Word default, but verify it.
↕️
Line Spacing
Double-spaced throughout — including the heading block, title, and body. No extra spaces between paragraphs.
↩️
Paragraph Indent
0.5 inch (one Tab) at the start of every body paragraph. Use Tab, not the space bar.
📅
Date Format
Write dates as Day Month Year — e.g., 14 March 2025. No commas.
🔖
No Title Page
MLA does not use a separate title page. All identifying information goes at the top of page one.
📖
Works Cited
Start on a new page after the body. Center the heading "Works Cited" — not bold, not italic. Entries are double-spaced with a hanging indent.
🔢
In-Text Citations
Place the author's last name and page number in parentheses after the quote: (Hurston 24). The period goes after the closing parenthesis.
⚠️ Common Formatting Mistakes to Avoid
✗
Adding a separate title page. MLA does not use one. Everything goes at the top of page one.
✗
Bolding, enlarging, or underlining your title. Your title uses the same font and size as the rest of the essay — no special formatting unless it contains a work's title.
✗
Adding extra blank lines after the title or between paragraphs. Double spacing is consistent throughout — no extra line breaks anywhere.
✗
Writing the date as March 14, 2025. MLA date order is Day Month Year — 14 March 2025 — with no comma.
✗
Forgetting the running head or putting it only on page 2+. Your last name and page number appear in the header on every page, including page one.
✗
Putting the period before the in-text citation. The period always comes after the closing parenthesis: "quote" (Author ##).
