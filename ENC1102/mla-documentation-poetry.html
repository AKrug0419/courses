Documentation Hub · Module Seven

MLA Documentation for the
Poetry Research Essay

Everything you need to format, cite, and integrate sources in the research essay, collected in one place.

The poetry research essay asks you to work with two kinds of sources at once: a primary source (the poem itself) and three secondary sources (scholarly articles and web reviews). Each kind has its own citation conventions, and integrating them into your prose requires more than just dropping in a quotation and moving on. The two resources below cover the rules you will use most often. Start with How to Quote from Poetry before you draft your body paragraphs, then return to Quoting from Your Secondary Research Sources as you weave your scholarly voices in.

Before You Draft

Read through both core resources before you begin writing your first body paragraph. Most citation errors in final drafts are not careless mistakes; they are the result of drafting first and checking the rules afterward. If you internalize the citation patterns now, you will write cleaner prose from the first sentence onward.
